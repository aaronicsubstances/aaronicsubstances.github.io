Psalm 29 (30 in Tanakh)
Song of Praise and Thanksgiving: Thou hast Healed Me
In His Will is Life and Security
1  (A Song for the Dedication of David’s Palace)
2  I will lift Thee on high, O Lord,
for Thou hast raised me up,
and hast not let my enemies rejoice over me.
3  O Lord, my God, I cried to Thee;
and Thou hast healed me.
4  O Lord, Thou hast brought up my soul from hell;
Thou hast saved me from those going down to the pit.
5  Sing to the Lord, O you His Saints,
and give thanks at the remembrance of His holiness.
6  For wrath is in His anger,†By angering and displeasing God, we incur wrath and retribution, ‘for the wages of sin is death’ (Rom. 6:23). By doing God’s will we find life, health and happiness (1 Jn. 2:17; 3:14; Rom. 14:17).
but in His will is life;
in the evening weeping may pitch its tent,
but joy comes with the morning.
7  So I said to myself in my prosperity:
‘I shall never be shaken.’
8  O Lord, in Thy love grant power to my beauty.‡‘Although I was beautiful by nature, I became feeble because I was deadened by sin through the malice of the serpent. So too the beauty I received from Thee when I was first created. Thou hast added power to do Thy will’ (St. Basil the Great).
But Thou didst turn away Thy face,
and I became troubled.
9  To thee, O Lord, I will cry,
and I will pray to my God:
10  ‘What profit is there in my blood,§Blood signifies death (cp. Lev. 17:14).
by my going down to corruption?
Will the dust give thanks to Thee,
or will it declare Thy truth?’
11  The Lord has heard and had mercy on me;
the Lord has become my helper.
12  Thou hast turned my mourning for me into joy;
Thou hast ripped off my sackcloth
and clothed me with gladness,
13  that my glory†glory: ‘The glory of a righteous man is the Spirit in him’ (St. Basil the Great). may sing to Thee,
and that I may not be pierced with sorrow.
O Lord my God, I will give thanks to Thee for ever.
