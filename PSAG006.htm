Psalm 6
A Cry in Anguish of Body and Soul
Faith Receives the Answer to Prayer
1  (A Psalm by David)
2  O Lord, rebuke me, but not in Thine anger;
and chasten me, but not in Thy wrath.†cp. Psalm 37:2; Jeremiah 10:24.
3  Have mercy on me, O Lord, for I am weak,
heal me, O Lord, for my bones shake.
4  My soul also is troubled exceedingly.
But Thou, O Lord, how long?‡Meaning: ‘But where art Thou, O Lord? How long am I to suffer?’ cp. Rev. 6:10. ‘How long, O sovereign Lord, holy and true, must it be before Thou wilt judge and punish the inhabitants of the earth for our blood?’
5  Return, O Lord, deliver my soul;
save me for Thy mercy’s sake.
6  For in death no one remembers Thee,
and who will confess Thee in hell?
7  I am weary and worn with my groaning.
Every night I bathe my bed
and sprinkle my couch with my tears.
8  My eye is troubled with anger;
I grow old among all my enemies.
9  Depart from me, all you who do evil,§cp. Mt. 7:23.
for the Lord has heard the voice of my weeping.
10  The Lord has heard my petition,
the Lord has received my prayer.
11  May all my enemies be ashamed and deeply troubled;
may they soon be routed and utterly confounded.
