Psalm 86 (87 in Tanakh)
The Celestial City of God, Mother of All Nations
The Kingdom of Righteousness, Peace and Joy
1  (For the sons of Korah. The Psalm of a song)
His foundations are on the holy mountains.
2  The Lord loves the gates of Zion†Zion is a type of the Church; the dwellings of Jacob signify life under the law (cp. St. Athanasius). Happy are they who are born of water and the Spirit and whose home is the heavenly Zion! (cp. Jn; 3:3-5). See also Ps. 136 and footnotes. ‘His foundations’ (Ephes. 2:20: Isaiah 28:16).
more than all the dwellings of Jacob.
3  Glorious things are said of you, O city of God.
(Pause)
4  I will mention Rahab‡Rahab (Heb.) = Pride. Mythical monger of chaos at creation (Job 24:12) and at redemption from Egypt (Is. 51:9). Poetic synonym for Egypt (Is. 30:7). and Babylon to those who know me.
and behold, foreigners and the people of Tyre and Ethiopia,
these were born there.
5  A man will say, ‘Mother Zion,’
and, ‘This man was born in her,’
and, ‘The Most High Himself founded her.’
6  The Lord will declare this in His record of the peoples
and rulers who were born in her.
(Pause)
7  How happy are all whose home is in you!§‘Whatever God now gives us, He himself will be to us in place of His gift.... Our joy, our peace, our rest, the end of all our troubles, is none but God’ (St. Augustine).
