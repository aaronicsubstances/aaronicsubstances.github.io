Psalm 110 (111 in Tanakh)
Praise and Thanksgiving for the Great Works of God
He sent Redemption to His People
1  (Alleluia)
I will thank Thee, O Lord, with my whole heart
in the assembly of the upright
and in the congregation.
2  Great are the works of the Lord,
adapted to all His purposes.
3  His work is filled with glory and majesty,
and His righteousness endures for ever.
4  He has made a memorial of His wonders;†Ex. 12:14-27; 1 Cor. 11:23-30; Mt. 26:28. This Psalm names some of the wonders: the manna, redemption from Egypt, the law-giving, the gift of the promised land.
merciful and compassionate is the Lord.
5  He gives food to those who fear Him;
He will ever be mindful of His covenant.‡Gen. 6:18; 9:9f; 15; 17; Ex. 19:5; Mk. 14:24; Lk. 22:20,29,30.
6  He has shown His people the power of His works
by giving them possession of the nations.
7  The works of His hands are truth and justice;
all His commandments are sure.
8  They are fixed throughout the ages;
they are made in truth and justice.
9  He sent redemption to His people;
He has commanded His covenant for ever.
Holy and terrible is His name.
10  The fear of the Lord is the beginning§Or: source, fount, spring, sum, essential principle (Prov. 1:7). of wisdom,
and all who do this have a good understanding.†To cultivate this fear is to practise living in the presence of God, which is the height of wisdom and understanding.
His praise continues throughout the ages.
