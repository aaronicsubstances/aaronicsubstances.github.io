Psalm 4
An Evening Prayer of Trust in God
The Sacrifice of Righteousness
1  (With harps. A Song of David)
2  Thou nearest me when I call,
O God of my righteousness;
Thou enlargest me in trouble.
Have mercy on me,
and hear my prayer.
3  Sons of men, how long will you be heavy-hearted?†heavy-hearted’: weighed down with earthly cares, instead of rising to divine contemplation (St. John Chrysostom. cp. Lk. 21:34).
Why do you love vanity and seek falsehood?
(Pause)
4  Know also that the Lord has made His Holy One wonderful;
the Lord will hear me when I cry to Him.
5  Be angry, yet do not sin;‡Ephes. 4:26. Be angry at sin, but love the sinner. ‘If you do give way to anger, lead it into silence with silent compunction of heart’ (St. Athanasius the Great).
for what you say in your hearts
feel compunction on your beds.§‘This relates to the widening of the heart required to receive the inpouring of love through the Holy Spirit’ (St. Augustine).
(Pause)
6  Offer the sacrifice of righteousness,†Obtain righteousness, do righteousness, and offer it in sacrifice to God’ (St. Athanasius). See also Ps. 16:1, 30:2 and footnotes there.
and put your trust in the Lord.
7  There are many who say, ‘Who will show us good times?’
The light of Thy presence has been signed upon us, O Lord.
8  Thou hast given my heart more gladness
than fills men at the harvest of their wheat, wine and oil.
9  In peace with Him I will rest and sleep;‡Or: I rest in peace and fall asleep at once.
for Thou, Lord, enablest me to live trustfully alone.§Following the Ethiopian version, which is based on the Septuagint.
