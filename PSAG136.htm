Psalm 136 (137 in Tanakh)
Song of the Babylonian Exile
The Unsung Song of the Heart
1  By the rivers of Babylon
we sat down and wept
when we remembered Zion.†Jerusalem and Zion are types of the Church, the bride of Christ. ‘Christ loved the Church and gave Himself up for her’ (Ephes. 5:25f).
2  On the willows in Babylon
we hung up our instruments.
3  For there our captors asked us for songs
and our abductors called for a tune, saying:
‘Sing us some of the songs of Zion.’
4  How can we sing the Lord’s song
in a foreign land?
5  If I forget you, O Jerusalem,
may my right hand be forgotten.
6  May my tongue stick in my throat
if I do not remember you,
if I do not put Jerusalem first‡See footnote on page 170.
as above all my joy.
7  Remember, O Lord, against the sons of Edom
the day of the fall of Jerusalem, when they said:
‘Down with it, down with it, to its very foundations!’
8  O daughter of Babylon the wretched!
Blessed is he who repays you for what you did to us.
Blessed is he who seizes your children
and dashes them against the rock.§Babylon signifies the flesh. Blessed is he who subdues it with fasts and deprives it of joys, as it dealt with the soul, and who dashes incipient evil thoughts, while still weak, against the Rock which is Christ (1 Cor. 10:4; Mt. 21:44; Rev. 18).
