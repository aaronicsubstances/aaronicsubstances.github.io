Psalm 27 (28 in Tanakh)
God the Protection and Salvation of His Anointed
A Prayer of Faith with Thanksgiving
(By David)
1  To Thee, O Lord, I cry.
O my God, be not silent to me;
lest if Thou be silent to me,
I become like those who go down to the pit.
2  Hear, O Lord, the cry of my prayer
when I pray to Thee,
when I lift up my hands
to Thy holy temple.
3  Do not drag away my soul with sinners
and destroy me not with wrongdoers,
who speak peace with their neighbour
while evil thoughts are in their hearts.
4  Give them, O Lord, according to their works,
and according to the evil of their ways;
give them according to the works of their hands,
repay them what they deserve.
5  Because they do not regard the works of the Lord
or the workings of His hands,†cp. Isaiah 5:12.
Thou wilt pull them down and not build them up.
6  Blessed is the Lord, for He has heard
the voice of my prayer.
7  The Lord is my helper and my protector;
on Him my heart relies and I am helped.
Even my flesh has revived,
and with all my heart I will give thanks to Him.
8  The Lord is the strength of His people,
and the protector and salvation of His anointed.
9  Save Thy people and bless Thy inheritance;
be their shepherd and carry them for ever.‡Cp. Isaiah 40:11; John 10:11.
