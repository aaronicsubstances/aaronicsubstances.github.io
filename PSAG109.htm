Psalm 109 (110 in Tanakh)
The Messiah King, Priest and Judge: His Triumphant Ascension
Ruling in the midst of His Enemies
1  (A Psalm by David)
The Lord said to my Lord: ‘Sit at My right hand,†‘Can you wonder that David’s Son is his Lord when you see that Mary was the Mother of her Lord? He is David’s Lord as being God the Lord of all and David’s Son as being the Son of Man. At once Lord and Son’ (St. Augustine. Cp. Mt. 22:43-45).
till I put Thy enemies under Thy feet.’
2  The Lord will send Thee from Zion the sceptre of power:‡The power of the Gospel, the power of the Cross, which is the power of the Holy Spirit given at Pentecost (St. Athanasius).
‘Rule in the midst of Thy enemies.’
3  The dominion is Thine in the day of Thy power,
in the glories of Thy saints.§Cp. 1 Cor. 15:41-43.
From the womb before the dawn I begot Thee.†Before the dawn of creation, before time began, the Son is eternally begotten of the Father. He never left His Father’s bosom even as a man on earth (Jn. 1:18; 3:13).
4  The Lord has sworn and will not change His mind:
‘Thou art a priest forever in the line of Melchieedek.’‡Heb. 7:21.
5  The Lord at Thy right hand
will crush kings in the day of His wrath.
6  He will judge among the nations,
He will fill the earth with corpses;
He will crush the heads of many on earth.
7  He will drink from the stream by the wayside;
therefore He will lift up His head.§‘Showing His active and frugal way of life, Christ spent most of His time in mountains and deserts, not only by day, but also by night’ (St. Chrysostom). Dt. 8:7. Stream: lit. torrent. Cp. Ps. 35:9.
