Psalm 11 (12 in Tanakh)
The Safety of the Poor and Needy
Faith in the Truth of God’s Promises
1  (A Psalm by David)
2  Save me, O Lord, for there is no saint left;
for truths have dwindled from the sons of men.
3  Empty follies they talk, everyone with his neighbor;
with lying lips they speak evil with double heart.
4  May the Lord destroy all lying lips,
a tongue that boasts and those who say:
5  ‘We will make more of our tongue.
Our lips are our own.
Who is lord over us?’
6  ‘Because of the oppression of the needy
and the groaning of the poor,
now I will arise,’ says the Lord;
‘I will set him in safety and speak plainly in him.’†cp. Mt. 10:19, 20; Luke 21:12-19.
7  The words of the Lord are pure words,
like silver refined by fire, purged of earth,
seven times purified.
8  Thou, O Lord, wilt keep us
and preserve us from this generation and for ever.
9  The godless prowl around;
from Thy height, great is Thy care of the sons of men.
